**Core problem:** Most children's content is designed for maximum engagement through overstimulation (bright colors, fast cuts, constant noise), which can be harmful to toddlers' developing brains and make parents feel guilty about screen time.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Cocomelon/mainstream kids content: Overstimulating, addictive, makes kids zoned out
- Educational apps: Too much screen interaction, not passive-friendly
- Adult music: Not designed for toddlers, lacks visual component

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Guilt about current content choices, seeing their child overstimulated or "zoned out," concerns about developmental impact, judgment from other parents or pediatricians

**Pull:** Promises of guilt-free screen time, community of like-minded intentional parents, actually joyful content (not boring), easy to access (YouTube/streaming)

**Habit:** Kids are already hooked on Cocomelon, existing routines built around current content, fear of toddler meltdowns during transition

**Anxiety:** "Will my kid actually watch this?", "What if they reject it and have a meltdown?", "Is this really better or just different marketing?"
